Illuminate Manufacturing Intelligence (IMI2.0)

For my last internship during undergrad school, I worked as a UX Software Developer (UI/UX Designer & Developer) at ATS Corporation. My team’s objective is to renovate the existing Illuminate Manufacturing Intelligence (IMI1.0), a software platform for Monitoring Machinary Productivity. I worked on upgrading the State Timeline page and several other pages. My goal is to make the design more intutive and increase and uphold brand identity across all web pages.

Identifying the Issues

01

Overwhelming Amount of Data

The large amount of data displayed on the chart might overwhelm some users, making it hard to focus on specific details.

02

Style Inconsistency

Inconsistent spacing and typography can lower the level of professionalism.

03

Missing branding

A lack of branding identity makes it impossible to recognize the product creator.

Solutions

01

Decreasing the amount of data on the chart and adding a horizontal scrollbar solved the issue of excessive data on display.

02

Previously, there was a sub-equipment button with a less-known icon and a calendar button. I used a highly recognizable filter button to replace these two buttons. Their functions were combined into the new filter. In addition, I added a bookmark button as instructed by my mentor.

03

The narrow and inconspicuous tabs were relocated to the left navigation bar and are now accompanied by icons to make them stand out. Their text is now in a consistent style. The areas encircled with blue are present on every web page of IMI2.0 for uniform branding.

Takeaways

01

Brand Identity Is Much Beyond A Logo

When I was first asked to add more branding, my initial thought was to add ATS Corporation's logo to every web page design. However, I soon asked myself: "Is this enough?". Then I proceeded to redesign the spacing, typography, color scheme, etc. until I felt strongly about my design. I realized branding should be much more than a logo. It should include consistent typography, interaction, look and feel.

02

Be Picky & Be Critical

I learned not to settle for the initial design even if it passed usability tests. Instead, I should ponder whether a design that aligns more with the requirements exists. If I were easily satisfied with the first good thing I made, a better design would not born.
