The problem

Many learners practice interviews alone or through static question banks. They often receive no feedback on language use, tone, or communication patterns—factors that shape real-world interview success.

The goal

Design a platform that not only simulates interviews but also analyzes verbal performance and guides improvement over time.

User research: summary

I conducted a competitor analysis of leading study and learning platforms like Quizlet, Brainscape, and Khan Academy to understand the current offerings in educational tools. How Insights Influenced Design Choices

Insight 1

Gap in Performance Feedback

Platforms like Quizlet and Brainscape provided useful learning modes and memory retention techniques but lacked in-depth performance feedback beyond simple correctness or flashcard repetition.

Insight 2

Need for Gamification to Keep Users Engaged

Brainscape’s flashcards feature periodic repetition, but there was no sense of progression or motivation beyond the content itself. Quizlet’s match mode attempted to add fun with ranking, but it didn’t engage users beyond a conceptual level.

Insight 3

Emphasis on Customization and Personalization

While Khan Academy offered a rich, in-depth experience, it often felt too text-heavy and lacked interactive, user-driven customization. This created friction for users who wanted more personalized feedback and engagement.

Design Choice 1

To address this, I introduced real-time performance feedback that analyzes not just correct answers but also how learners respond in terms of language quality, tone, and clarity. The platform evaluates common challenges like repetition of words, use of filler words, and timing of pauses, and provides actionable suggestions to improve. This made the learning experience more dynamic and personalized, catering to users looking for continuous improvement rather than static results.

Design Choice 2

Drawing from this, I designed progress tracking features that show users their completion rate and performance scores in real-time, presented as graphs and charts. Additionally, the “Good Job” and “Need Improvement” visual cues directly addressed users’ need for gamified, motivational feedback. This feature mirrors competitive ranking, similar to Quizlet’s match mode, but with deeper contextual relevance, such as progress in behavioral, technical, and situational question categories.

Design Choice 3

This screen lets users choose from different interview categories—behavioral, technical, situational, and brainteasers, so they can practice based on their priorities. It gives users control over what they want to improve, adding flexibility without overwhelming them with settings.

The progress dashboard personalizes the experience by showing each user’s improvement over time, turning their performance data into actionable insights. It visualizes metrics such as completion rate and confidence scores, making the experience feel specific to the individual.

Prototype Testing

To validate the effectiveness of Radical AI, I conducted prototype testing with 12 participants from diverse backgrounds, including early-career professionals and mid-level UX designers. The goal was to gather feedback on the core features, such as the mock interview process, real-time AI feedback, and performance tracking.

Testing Methodology

Participants

12 participants, aged 22-35, preparing for UX and tech interviews, with a mix of novice and intermediate-level skills.

Data Collection

Feedback was gathered through a combination of: Surveys: To measure user satisfaction and perceived confidence improvements. User Interviews: To capture qualitative insights about their experience with navigation, feedback interpretation, and overall usability.

Key Metrics and Feedback

92% User Satisfaction

Out of the 12 participants, 11 were highly satisfied with the platform, praising its user-friendly interface, the clarity of the performance feedback, and the ease of navigation.

83% Improved Confidence

10 participants reported feeling more confident in their ability to answer interview questions after receiving personalized feedback, especially regarding word choice, tone, and pacing.

75% Would Recommend

9 participants indicated they would recommend the platform to their peers, citing its value in preparing for UX and tech interviews through realistic practice and insightful AI feedback.

Key Insights from Testing

Real-Time Feedback

Participants appreciated the immediate, actionable feedback after each session, particularly for its ability to identify filler words and offer alternative suggestions for stronger phrasing.

Progress Tracking

The progress dashboard was particularly well-received, as it allowed users to track their improvements and feel motivated throughout their practice sessions.

Tone of AI Feedback

Users found the tone of the AI feedback to be supportive and encouraging, which helped mitigate the intimidation often felt when interacting with AI-driven platforms.

Takeaways

User-Centered Design Drives Success

The emphasis on user feedback was crucial throughout the design process. Engaging with participants early and often ensured that every design decision was grounded in real user needs and expectations. By focusing on user satisfaction (92%) and understanding how real-time feedback and progress tracking impacted their confidence, I learned how to prioritize features that resonate with users. In the future, continuous feedback and usability testing would be a key strategy for validating and iterating on designs before launch.

Balance Between Functionality and Emotional Experience

The emotional aspect of the user experience was just as important as the functionality. Creating a platform that feels supportive, motivational, and encouraging helped users feel more comfortable and confident while interacting with AI. This balanced approach, where data-driven feedback is paired with an empathetic tone, was critical in making the platform feel like a coaching tool rather than a cold, impersonal evaluator. Moving forward, I’ll continue to focus on the emotional side of UX design, especially when working on AI-driven platforms, where user trust and engagement are key.
